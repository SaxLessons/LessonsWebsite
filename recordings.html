Saxophone Studio

Sonata for Saxohpone by Bernhard Heiden:

Bernhard Heiden's Sonata for Alto Saxophone and Piano was his very first published work. It is now one of the most performed pieces of saxophone repertoire ever; it was the first sonata to become a standard part of collegiate alto saxophone repertoire and is considered a cornerstone for classical saxophone performance. This work is very instrumentally conceived, and not at all in vocal style. The sonata has three movements: Allegro in G major, Vivace in C minor, and Adagio-Presto in G major. The sonata is polyphonic; while it is written for solo saxophone, both piano part and saxophone part have independent melodic lines throughout the piece.
- Notes by Tyler Cosley

Due to time constraints for the concert this was recorded from, I only performed the first two movements. There were many things that I learned from the experience of practicing and performing this piece. I hope that, even with the mixing, that you can enjoy this piece as much as I liked learning it!

Sonata for Baritone Saxophone and Piano by Sherwood Shaffer

Sonata for Baritone Saxophone and Piano was composed in the late fall of persents a bold main theme that is later contrasted by a "music box" more lyrical theme with a fugal last part before both themes return in a quasi-sonata form; the slow movement is a three-part song form with a cadenza for the saxophone that explores some of the altissimo register of the instrument before the concluding phrase of the first theme; the rondo finale alternates two quick-driving themes with a quieter, more lyrical theme plus a short cadenza with [the] altissimo register before flying downward into the lowest register for the end.
- Program notes by composer, additions added by Shayden Hawkins

For this concert, because of multiple time constraints I only learned the first movement of this piece I would personally say this was not my best playing but I'm grateful to Dr. Gao to giving me the opportunity to perform this lovely piece and I hope that it is still enjoyable piece to listen to me perform.
